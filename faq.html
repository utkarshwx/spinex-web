Frequently Asked Questions

Need something cleared up? Here are our most frequently asked questions.

What is Pain Management?

Pain management is a specialized field of medicine dedicated to helping people find relief from pain. It takes a comprehensive approach, addressing both the physical cause of pain and its impact on a person's overall well-being. This can involve a range of treatments, from medication and therapy to minimally invasive procedures and rehabilitation programs. The goal is to improve quality of life by reducing pain and helping individuals regain function and return to their daily activities.

What is Interventional Pain Management?

Interventional pain management offers a targeted approach to pain relief. It utilizes image-guided injections, often using X-ray or CT scans, to deliver medication directly to the source of your pain. This can include injections into joints or the discs between spinal bones. In many cases, interventional pain management can help patients avoid the need for surgery.

What is a Pain Centre?

A Pain Center offers a comprehensive approach to chronic pain management. These centers go beyond just pain relief, focusing on empowering patients to regain control of their lives. Through minimally invasive treatments and a multidisciplinary approach, pain centers aim to minimize disruption to your daily routine and promote a quicker recovery compared to surgical options.

What kinds of Pain are treated?

Pain manifests in many forms, with some of the most frequent being Orthopedic Pain, Spondilitis, Shoulder & Elbow Pain, Chronic Back Pain, Diabetic Neuropathy, Cancer Pain, Headache and Migraine, Osteoarthritis & Rheumatoid arthritis, Post-Neurological Pain and Sports Injury..

What is a Pain Centre?

Minimally invasive injections deliver medication directly to inflamed tissues around spinal structures caused by herniated discs. The most common procedures include:

  • Epidural injections: Target nerves near the spinal cord for pain relief.
  • Facet and sacroiliac joint injections: Address pain originating from specific spinal joints.
  • Spinal cord stimulator: Uses electrical pulses to disrupt pain signals near the spinal cord.
  • Intrathecal pumps: Continuously deliver pain medication directly into the spinal fluid.

    • Are these procedures safe?

    Concerns exist among the crowd regarding the safety of these procedures. However, when performed by a skilled and experienced doctor, they are safe and painless.

    Are these treatment successful?

    When performed by skilled professionals using X-ray guidance, these treatments can be very effective in managing pain. Following the procedures, patients typically participate in a rehabilitation program. This program may include relaxation techniques, exercises you can perform at home, and adjustments to your daily routine. In most cases, these treatments offer excellent long-term success rates.
