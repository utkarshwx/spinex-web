Neck Pain


Neck pain, also called cervical pain, is a frequent issue affecting two-thirds of the population at some point. Women are slightly more susceptible than men. While most cases resolve within a year, some people experience persistent pain and disability.


Causes of neck pain
Nerve Compression: Herniated discs or bone spurs in the neck vertebrae can press on nerves, causing pain.
Muscle Strain: Poor posture, overuse (like hunching over devices), or even minor activities like reading in bed can strain neck muscles.
Age-Related Wear and Tear: Just like other joints, the neck experiences wear and tear with age, leading to osteoarthritis.
Injuries:Whiplash from car accidents is a common cause of neck pain.
Diseases: In rare cases, neck pain can be a symptom of an underlying condition like rheumatoid arthritis, meningitis, or cancer.

Symptoms to Watch For

Neck pain often presents as a dull ache that worsens with movement. Other symptoms can include:

  • • Numbness
  • • Tingling
  • • Tenderness
  • • Sharp pain
  • • Difficulty swallowing
  • • Dizziness


Seeking Medical Attention:

Seek immediate medical attention for any neck injury. If the pain is:

  • • Constant and persistent
  • • Severe
  • • Radiates down the arms or legs
  • • Accompanied by headaches, numbness, tingling, or weakness


Treatment Options

Management of neck pain depends on the underlying cause. Here's an overview of common treatments:

Acute Pain: Home remedies like heat/ice therapy and over-the-counter pain relievers are often helpful. If ineffective, consult a pain specialist for further evaluation.
Chronic Pain: For persistent pain, a pain specialist can develop a personalized treatment plan based on the specific cause.
Physical Therapy: Techniques like ultrasound, exercises, and controlled traction can help strengthen neck muscles and improve flexibility. (Exercises are avoided during acute pain.)
Medications: A combination of pain relievers, nerve medications, and muscle relaxants may be prescribed.
Interventional Pain Management: If conservative approaches fail to provide relief, injections like cervical epidural and trigger point injections can be highly effective and safe when performed by a pain specialist.
Lifestyle Modifications: Maintaining good posture, avoiding heavy lifting, and limiting prolonged phone use are crucial for preventing future episodes.
Surgery: Surgery is rarely necessary and only considered for specific cases, not solely for pain management.
